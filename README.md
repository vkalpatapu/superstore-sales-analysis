# Superstore Sales EDA — Retail Analytics

## Business Problem
As a data analyst at a retail company, analyze 10,000 sales transactions to identify profitability gaps, underperforming regions, and loss-making product categories to support data-driven business decisions.

## Dataset
- **Source:** Kaggle — Superstore Dataset
- **Size:** 9,994 rows | 21 columns
- **Period:** Multi-year US retail sales data

## Tools Used
- **Excel** — Pivot Tables, SUMIF, Conditional Formatting
- **PostgreSQL** — 5 business queries
- **Power BI** — Interactive dashboard with conditional color formatting
- **Python** — Pandas EDA, Matplotlib visualization

## Key Findings
- Technology is the top revenue category at $836K (36% of total)
- California alone drives $457K — 20% of total revenue (concentration risk)
- Central region has only 7.92% profit margin vs West at 14.94%
- Tables sub-category losing $17,725 annually — active money loser
- Home Office segment has highest average order value at $240

## Business Recommendations
- Review Central region discounting strategy immediately
- Discontinue or reprice Tables and Bookcases
- Target Home Office segment for upselling — fewer orders but higher spend
- Expand geographic presence to reduce California concentration risk
